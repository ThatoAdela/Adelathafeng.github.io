Our Stakeholders

IWB is proud to be supported by a diverse group of investors committed to environmental sustainability and technological innovation in Lesotho.

Kenneth

Co-Founder & CEO

Ownership: 40%

A technology entrepreneur with over 15 years of experience in the IT industry. Kenneth founded IWB in 2024 with a vision to address the growing e-waste problem in Lesotho while creating sustainable jobs in the technology sector.

Shadrack

CEO

Ownership: 20%

Shadrack is an experienced leader in the recycling and waste management industry, known for his commitment to circular economy solutions and environmental sustainability.

With a background in business management and environmental science, he has successfully guided IWB recycling company toward innovation and growth in waste management sector.

Lineo Makhotla

Chief Technology Officer

Ownership: 15%

With a background in computer engineering and environmental science, Lineo leads the development of IWB's innovative recycling processes and software tools. She previously worked for international technology companies before returning to Lesotho.

National University of Lesotho

Institutional Investor

Ownership: 10%

The National University of Lesotho supports IWB through its Innovation and Technology Transfer Office. This partnership provides research support and connects IWB with talented graduates in computer science and environmental engineering.

Lesotho Innovation Fund

Investment Group

Ownership: 10%

A government-backed initiative that invests in innovative local businesses. The fund provides capital and business development support to help IWB scale its operations throughout the country.

Mokhele Ramatlapeng

Angel Investor

Ownership: 5%

A successful businessman and philanthropist who is passionate about environmental conservation. Mokhele provides mentorship and strategic guidance to IWB's management team.
